As Blood Runs Black- Intro

You might wonder what posessed me to review a song that comes in under one minute. To be honest, that's a very good question, and the answer is "A friend told me to." Granted, I suppose I sitll bear some of the responsibility given that I picked the songs by scrolling at massive speeds through my spotify playlist and getting my friends to point to songs.

That said, I take no issue with having to review this song. It definitely punches well above it's weight class, doing a great job of introducing the ABloRB sound to a new listener in a concise and approachable manner. From the heavy, chug burst-packed breakdowns to the frantic tremolo picking and the unending obsession with harmonic minor scale, everything about perfectly represents what I have come to expect from them. What's more, the harmonic minor scale is used in such a way that it creates the feeling that there is tension in the air, perfectly beffiting the theme of political rebllion tying together most of the album.

From a more traditional corekid standpoint, I feel obliged to discuss the breakdown. The breakdown is heavy by all standards, but considering it as a one-minute insrumental song with no buildup or makeshift callout: It's nothing short of a major achievement. Just under 2 decades later, even modern instrumental hits like Sparrow- as much of a hit as an instrumental metal song can be, that is- simply fail to transition so smoothly and coherently as Intro does.

In other words, no song will ever match Intro in being objctively amazing and acheving everything and more it set out to: While being isntrumental and under a minute. Big claps for ABRB.
